Bioluminescent Wonders

The Ocean's Inner Light

In the ocean, blue-green is the most common color of bioluminescence because these wavelengths travel best through water.

Organisms use bioluminescence for various purposes: attracting prey, avoiding predators, attracting mates, and communication. For example, anglerfish use a bioluminescent lure, and some squid release glowing mucus to confuse threats.
