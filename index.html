Deep Sea Mysteries

\n
\n

The Mariana Trench is the deepest oceanic trench on Earth, reaching a maximum-known depth of 10,984 meters (36,037 feet) at the Challenger Deep. If Mount Everest were placed in the trench, its peak would still be over a mile below the surface!

Did You Know?

\n

The pressure at the bottom of the Mariana Trench is over 1,000 times the standard atmospheric pressure at sea level. Despite this, unique forms of life, like amphipods and sea cucumbers, thrive in this extreme environment!
