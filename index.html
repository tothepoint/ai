Oceanic Depths

The Deepest Point on Earth

The Mariana Trench is the deepest oceanic trench on Earth, reaching a maximum-known depth of about 11,000 meters (36,000 feet).

If Mount Everest were placed in the Trench, its peak would still be over a mile underwater. This extreme environment is home to unique life forms adapted to immense pressure and perpetual darkness.
