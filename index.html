The Unfathomable Black Hole

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing—no particles or even electromagnetic radiation such as light—can escape from it.

The boundary of no escape is called the event horizon. Beyond this point, all paths lead inward towards the singularity, a point of infinite density. Black holes are formed from the remnants of massive stars that collapse at the end of their life cycle.
